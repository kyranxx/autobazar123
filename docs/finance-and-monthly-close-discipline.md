# Finance And Monthly Close Discipline

This document is step 7 of the company sale readiness plan.

Its purpose is to define the minimum financial discipline Autobazar123 should have if it wants to be run well and be easier to sell later.

In simple words:

- the company should know its numbers
- the numbers should be finalized regularly
- finance and analytics should not tell different stories

## Why This Matters

Many founders know traffic and product metrics well but know finance weakly.

That becomes a problem because buyers care about:

- revenue quality
- margin quality
- cash discipline
- repeatability

If finance is messy, even a good product can look risky.

## What Monthly Close Means

Monthly close means:

- finalizing the month
- reconciling important numbers
- recording what is true for that month

This should happen every month, not only at year end.

## What Should Be Finalized Monthly

### Revenue

Know:

- subscription revenue
- premium placement revenue
- one-time revenue
- refunds
- discounts
- recognized revenue

Why:
Revenue quality matters more than just top-line growth.

### Cash

Know:

- starting cash
- ending cash
- monthly burn
- runway

Why:
Cash discipline is basic management discipline.

### Receivables

Know:

- what customers owe
- what is overdue
- which accounts are risky

Why:
Revenue is less useful if it is not actually collected.

### Expenses

Know:

- payroll
- contractors
- hosting and software
- marketing spend
- other major operating costs

Why:
This shows whether growth is efficient.

## The Monthly Finance Questions

Every month, the founders should be able to answer:

- How much real revenue did we generate?
- How much cash did we end with?
- How much did we burn?
- Which customers paid?
- Which customers are late?
- Did refunds or discounts increase?
- Did finance and analytics tell the same story?

If the team cannot answer these clearly, financial discipline is weak.

## Finance Source Of Truth Rule

Use a simple rule:

- product analytics explains behavior
- finance explains recognized money

They should support each other, but finance should be the source of truth for revenue reporting.

## Minimum Monthly Close Checklist

At month end:

- reconcile payment records
- reconcile invoices
- review refunds
- review discounts or credits
- confirm ending cash
- update burn and runway
- save the monthly snapshot
- update the founder / board pack

## Revenue Quality In Plain Language

Revenue quality means:

- the money is real
- the customer actually pays
- it is not inflated by one-off exceptions
- it is not hiding refund or discount problems

Use case:
If reported revenue is high but many invoices are unpaid or heavily discounted, the quality of earnings is weaker.

## Good Financial Habits

### Keep Categories Stable

Use stable categories for:

- subscription revenue
- premium products
- one-time services
- refunds
- discounts

Why:
This makes month-to-month comparison easier.

### Separate Company And Personal Spending

Founders should keep personal and company money separate.

Why:
This is basic hygiene and reduces accounting confusion.

### Track Credits And Exceptions

If the business gives:

- credits
- discounts
- manual refunds
- special pricing

track them clearly.

Why:
These items directly affect revenue quality.

### Review Late Payments

Do not ignore overdue invoices.

Why:
This affects cash quality and customer risk.

## Metrics Finance Should Support

Finance should support or validate:

- paying dealers
- average revenue per dealer
- subscription revenue
- refund rate
- dealer revenue retention
- cash runway

This does not mean finance owns every business metric.

It means finance helps make the revenue story believable.

## What Buyers Care About

Buyers often care less about pretty dashboards and more about questions like:

- Is revenue recurring?
- Is revenue concentrated?
- Are margins improving?
- Are collections healthy?
- Are discounts unusually high?
- Do the books match the reported story?

If the answers are clear, trust goes up.

That is good for running the company now and important for selling it later.
