# Diligence Room Structure

This document is step 8 of the company sale readiness plan.

Its purpose is to define how Autobazar123 should organize important company materials so fundraising or acquisition diligence is easier later.

In simple words:

- a diligence room is an organized evidence folder
- it helps outsiders understand the company quickly
- it reduces panic when a serious process starts

## Status

- Step 8 completed: diligence room structure defined here
- The initial company sale readiness sequence is now complete

## Why This Matters

When a buyer starts diligence, they ask for many documents quickly.

If the company already has a structure, the process is much easier.

If documents are scattered everywhere, the process becomes slow, stressful, and error-prone.

## Main Rule

Keep important records organized before you need them.

Do not wait until a sale process starts.

## Suggested Top-Level Folder Structure

### `00_Company_Overview`

Put:

- company summary
- org chart
- product summary
- latest KPI summary
- latest founder / board pack

Why:
This gives outsiders a fast orientation.

### `01_Corporate`

Put:

- incorporation documents
- bylaws or equivalent
- board approvals
- shareholder approvals
- stock records
- option records
- cap table

Why:
This shows company formation and ownership history.

### `02_Finance`

Put:

- monthly P&L
- balance sheet
- cash flow
- budgets
- tax filings
- bank summaries
- finance policies if any

Why:
This supports financial diligence.

### `03_Commercial`

Put:

- dealer agreements
- customer terms
- key partnerships
- pricing summaries
- major account summaries

Why:
This shows how the company actually makes money.

### `04_Product_And_Data`

Put:

- analytics foundation spec
- metric dictionary
- analytics event spec
- monthly snapshots
- founder / board packs
- key dashboards

Why:
This gives a buyer a clean picture of company performance.

### `05_Tech_And_Operations`

Put:

- architecture overview
- infrastructure map
- monitoring summary
- incident log
- backup notes
- access control summary

Why:
This reduces fear of hidden technical fragility.

### `06_Security_And_Privacy`

Put:

- privacy policy
- cookie policy
- data map
- vendor list
- processor agreements
- retention rules
- security incident history

Why:
Privacy and security issues can slow or damage transactions.

### `07_IP_And_Brand`

Put:

- trademarks
- domain ownership records
- logo ownership
- employee IP assignments
- contractor IP assignments

Why:
This helps prove the company owns what it is selling.

### `08_HR`

Put:

- employment agreements
- contractor agreements
- compensation summaries
- option grant summaries
- organization chart

Why:
This helps explain people risk and team structure.

### `09_Legal`

Put:

- material contracts
- disputes or claims
- regulatory issues
- standard legal templates

Why:
This gives a buyer visibility into legal exposure.

### `10_Diligence_FAQ`

Put:

- short written answers to repeated buyer questions

Examples:

- How is sale confirmation measured?
- Who owns the code?
- What share of revenue comes from top dealers?
- Which systems are founder dependent?

Why:
This speeds diligence and avoids repeating the same explanation many times.

## Good Diligence Room Habits

### Keep The Structure Stable

Do not keep reorganizing folders.

Why:
Consistency saves time later.

### Name Files Clearly

Use simple names like:

- `2026-03-founder-board-pack.md`
- `2026-03-monthly-snapshot.md`
- `dealer-master-agreement-template.pdf`

Why:
Good naming reduces confusion.

### Keep Versions

Do not overwrite important historical materials.

Why:
Older versions can matter in diligence.

### Keep Sensitive Materials Controlled

Some materials should be access-controlled.

Why:
A diligence room should be organized, but still handled carefully.

## What This Looks Like In Practice

A simple internal diligence room could look like:

```text
diligence-room/
  00_Company_Overview/
  01_Corporate/
  02_Finance/
  03_Commercial/
  04_Product_And_Data/
  05_Tech_And_Operations/
  06_Security_And_Privacy/
  07_IP_And_Brand/
  08_HR/
  09_Legal/
  10_Diligence_FAQ/
```

This can start as a local or cloud folder long before any real sale process exists.
